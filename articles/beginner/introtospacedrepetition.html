Repetition is good, but spaced repetition is even better. Spaced repetition involves, as the name implies, spacing out repetition of material. An example of this would be to learn a subtopic, revise it 2 days later, then 5 days later, then 12 days later, and so onâ€¦

How can you use Spaced Repetition?

Thatâ€™s the basic essence of spaced repetition, but how can you (busy IB student) use it in your busy IB life? The answer to that is, spaced repetition can help you out in every single subject. Say you just learned a bunch of new words in Spanish, hereâ€™s how to use spaced repetition to your advantage:

  • Learn the words on the first day (duh).
  • 2 days later, go over the words again and try to remember them.
  • 5 days later, go over the words again.
  • 12 days later, go over the same words again.
  • Keep expanding the time intervals according to the ease of the content.
    • If you find it too easy, make the next revision interval longer.
    • If you find it too difficult, make the next interval shorter.

And this is the secret behind studying in an easier, faster and more effective way.
